Registration Steps

  • Register at the ACM-ICPC Baylor System HERE. The registration deadline is now extended to 10/25.
  • Complete the registration form HERE.
  • Waiting for the Email reply from us (the Email will be sent to your contact Email address provided in the form in the previous step) to see whether if the registration qualification of your team has been confirmed.
  • Complete the billing information HERE. Payment options include credit card (for foreign teams outside of Taiwan) and major convenient stores (for local teams from Taiwan).
  • Complete the team information form HERE to let us know your needs of meals, accommodation, Visa application, etc.
  • If you need a formal invitation letter to apply Taiwan Visa, please send us your team name, names of all participants (if needed, National Identification Card IDs as well), and other special needs (such as the date of staying, which will be 11/25-11/27 by default) to official mail. In addition, if any other documents are needed, please also kindly feel free to inform, and we will manage that for you ASAP.
  • Following the instructions given in the confirmation Email in the previous step, pay the registration fees (USD 150 or NTD 4500 with accommodation, and NTD 2500 without accommodation) through credit card (for foreign teams outside Taiwan) or convenient store (for local teams in Taiwan).
  • You are all done! Welcome to ACM-ICPC Chung-Li Regional Contest!

Qualifications for Contest Registration (in Descending Order of Priority)

Chinese Version:

English Version:

(If the maximal capacity is reached, all classes of lower priorities cannot participate. Also note that a team will lose its priorities if they changed any team member.)
  • Class 1: 15 teams will be selected from the 2016 National Computer Programming Contest (NCPC) in Taiwan (each university can have no more than 2 teams in this class). If there is any team in this class that cannot participate, lower-ranked teams can fill the vacancies.
  • Class 2: 5 teams will be selected from the 2016 National Contest for Private Universities (NCPU), and another 5 teams will be selected from the National Contest for Technology Universities (NCTU). Each university can have no more than 2 teams in this class.
  • Class 3: 5 teams will be selected from the 2016 Information Technology Software Academy (ITSA) Programming Contest in Taiwan. Each university can have no more than 2 teams in this class.
  • Class 4: Teams ranked 40th or better in the 2016 National Computer Programming Contest (NCPC) in Taiwan (each university can have no more than 2 teams in this class) will be selected. If the host university of the ACM-ICPC regional contest has not selected 2 teams in Classes 1 ~ 3, it has a priority to select its best 2 teams to register.
  • Class 5: For universities without any team yet, each of them can select 1 team to register, provided that the selected team has solved at least 1 problem in the NCPC or ACM ICPC Taiwan Online Programming Contest in the same year. This class will alternatively select teams from the qualified universities from NCPC or previous ACM ICPC Taiwan Online Programming Contest.
  • Class 6: The registration priorities of all other teams are based on the order of registration at the ACM-ICPC Baylor system.
