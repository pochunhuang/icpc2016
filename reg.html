Registration Steps

  • Register at the ACM-ICPC Baylor System HERE.
  • Complete the registration form HERE.
  • Waiting for the Email reply from us (the Email will be sent to your contact Email address provided in the form in the previous step) to see whether if the registration qualification of your team has been confirmed.
  • Complete the billing information HERE. Payment options include credit card (for foreign teams outside of Taiwan) and major convenient stores (for local teams from Taiwan).
  • Complete the team information form NOT ANNOUNCED YET to let us know your needs of meals, accommodation, Visa application, etc.
  • Following the instructions given in the confirmation Email in the previous step, pay the registration fees (USD 150 or NTD 4500 with accommodation, and NTD 2500 without accommodation) through credit card (for foreign teams outside Taiwan) or convenient store (for local teams in Taiwan).
  • You are all done! Welcome to ACM-ICPC Chung-Li Regional Contest!

[The Following Limitations Apply Only For Local Teams in Taiwan]

Qualifications for Contest Registration (in Descending Order of Priority)

(If the maximal capacity is reached, all classes of lower priorities cannot participate. Also note that a team will lose its priorities if they changed any team member.)
  • The first 15 teams of the 2016 National Computer Programming Contest (NCPC) in Taiwan (each university can have no more than 2 teams in this class).
  • The first 5 teams of the 2016 National Contest for Private Universities (NCPU) in Taiwan (each university can have no more than 2 teams in this class).
  • The first 5 temas of the 2016 Information Technology Software Academy (ITSA) Programming Contest in Taiwan (each university can have no more than 2 teams in this class).
  • If the host university has not selected 2 teams in the first four classes, it can have a priority to fill up the quota until 2 teams.
  • The first 40 teams of the 2016 National Computer Programming Contest (NCPC) in Taiwan (each university can have no more than 2 teams in this class).
  • For universities without any team yet, each of them can elect 1 team to participate, provided that the elected team correctly solved at least one problem in NCPC or previous ACM-ICPC regional contest. This class will alternatively select one team from qualified universities from NCPC or previous ACM-ICPC.
  • The priorities of all other teams are based on the order of registration at the ACM-ICPC Baylor system.
